# Application Specification: The Sovereign Core
**Version: 1.0.0-Pre-Release**

## 1. Visual Design System
*   **Typography:** Space Grotesk (Bold/Black) for headers; Inter for body; JetBrains Mono for system metrics and identity status.
*   **Aesthetic:** "High-Intensity Monochrome" - Utilizing high-contrast black/white backgrounds with an accent of `#FF4B2B` (Sovereign Red).
*   **Principles:** Architectural honesty, brutalist borders, and purposeful motion via `motion/react`.

## 2. Implemented Components

### A. The Sovereign Core Hero
*   Dynamic text-stroke overlays.
*   Identity-first messaging: "Your Narrative. Your Power. Your Future."

### B. Education Portal (The Genre Hub)
*   **Selectable Genres:** Anime, Reality TV, Biographical, Drama, AI-Generated.
*   **Mechanism:** Maps common entertainment tropes to radical self-advocacy scenarios.
*   **State:** Integrated selection state for Genre and Topic.

### C. Instagram Check-in (Interactive)
*   Two-stage interactive story layout.
*   **Component 1 (Poll):** Collective data gathering on power definitions.
*   **Component 2 (Slider):** Personal sovereignty check-in.
*   **Action:** Native Web Share API integration.

### D. Anti-Paternalism Toolkit
*   **Advocacy Translator:** Re-coding internalizing language ("I need help") into rights-based demands ("I am exercising my right").
*   **System Power Map:** Visual hierarchy of systemic power from Federal (HUD) to Operational (Shelter Management).

### E. The Radicle (Demand Center)
*   A space for non-incrementalist demands.
*   Animates and persists user-driven "Unimagined Change" input.

### F. Crisis Hub (SOS & Support)
*   **SOS Gate:** Immediate reporting for systemic harm.
*   **Peer Den:** Anonymous chat interface.
*   **Mirror Rep:** A mirrored digital representation of trauma-informed insights.

## 3. Measurable Success Aspects
1.  **Shift in Language:** Tracked via "Translator" interactions.
2.  **Resource Link-throughs:** Clicks on System Map contact nodes.
3.  **Mobilization:** Participation in "Demand Action" submissions.
